The Hawk Eye
COLUMBUS JUNCTION -- The driver of a car was killed Saturday after he was thrown from his car after it left the road three miles northwest of Columbus Junction.
Timothy L. Farrier, 22, Columbus Junction, was southbound on Louisa County Road X-17 about 5 a.m. when he lost control on a curve and went into a ditch.
The Iowa State Patrol said Farrier's car struck a driveway and became airborne. When it landed, the car rolled and came to a stop on its top.
Farrier and a passenger in the car, Eric E. Farrier, 20, Columbus Junction, were thrown from the car. Neither man was wearing a seat belt and the state police's report said it was unknown if it would have made a difference.
Eric Farrier was taken to University of Iowa Hospitals. An official there late Saturday evening said the family was not releasing any information about his condition.
The state patrol did not indicate whether alcohol was involved in the crash.

Obituaries: 6/13/2000
Timothy Farrier
Timothy L. Farrier, 22, of Columbus Junction, died Saturday, June 10, 2000, as the result of an automobile accident west of Columbus Junction.
Born March 1, 1978, in Des Moines, he was the son of Ron and Julie Edwards Farrier.
Mr. Farrier worked at Hon in Muscatine, farmed with his parents and had hauled grain for area farmers.
He was a 1997 graduate of Columbus High School and earned an associate's degree in farm management from Muscatine Community College.
Survivors include his parents; two brothers, Eric and Nathan Farrier, at home; one sister, Katie Farrier, at home; his paternal grandmother, Lois Farrier of Ainsworth; his maternal grandparents, Bill and Helen Edwards of Ainsworth; and several aunts, uncles and cousins.
He was preceded in death by his paternal grandfather.
Visitation will be from 4 to 8 p.m. today at Stacy-Lewis Funeral Home in Columbus Junction.
The funeral for Mr. Farrier will be at 3:30 p.m. Wednesday at United Presbyterian Church in Columbus Junction. Burial will be in ColumbusCity Cemetery.
A memorial has been established.
