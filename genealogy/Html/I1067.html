Mt. Pleasant News July 18, 1964
Zylpha A. Hannah
Taken By Death
Mrs. Zylpha A. Hannah, 82,died Friday night at the Memorial hospital after an illness of the past week.
Zylpha Hannah was born October 31, 1881 in Jackson township, Henry county, the daughter of Ruth Scarborough and Perry Byers. She had lived all of her life in this community, attended Howe's Academy and taught school before her marriage. She was a member of the Donaldson Chapel Methodist Church, but had transferred to the Salem Methodist Church.
She is survived by four daughters: Mrs. Hamilton (Florence) Williams of Mt. Pleasant, Mrs. Fred (Virginia) Perrenoud of Winfield, Mrs. Charles A. (Ruth) Hoffman of Minot, N. D., and Mrs. Dale (Marcia) Cammack of Salem: by 14 grandchildren and six great-grandchildren, a brother, Harvey Byers of Hillsboro and a sister, Mrs. Fred Strieb of Longview, Wash.
Two sons, Robert Wayne and Benjamin Millard, preceded her in death.
Funeral services will be held at 2:30 p.m. Monday, July 20, at the Salem Methodist church. The Reverends, Walter Wellington, pastor ofthe Salem Methodist church, and Bonifucio Bb. Mequi will officiate.
Interment will be in South Salem Cemetery.
Friends may call at the R. K. Crane Funeral Home as they desire.
Friends who desire, may contribute to a memorial to the Salem Methodist Church in memory of Mrs. Hannah.
