Salem News August 21, 1924
COMMUNITY SERVICE
The community service held at the Methodist church last Sunday evening brought out a good attendance. The sermon by Rev. D.S. Staebler of Mt. Pleasant was an impressive presentation of prayer and its answers, and it was listened to with much interest. Mr. Leo Hannah gave a fine violin solo which was greatly appreciated.
The service next Sunday evening will be held in the Congregational church at 8 o'clock. Special music will be rendered by the Gospel Team quartet and Rev. Sams.
All are invited to attend this service.

Mt. Pleasant News, April 12, 1984
Leo Hannah
Leo Hannah, 85, Burlington, died at Klein Hospital, Burlington, Wednesday, April 11, 1984.
He was born Aug. 12, 1898, the son of Robert and Edith Temple Hannah. He married Gertrude Mattern on Dec. 19,1917, in Donnellson.
Mr. Hannah was a farmer and worked at the I.A.A.P. he was a 50-year Mason Lodge No. 17 of Salem and a member of Fraternal Order of Eagles No. 150.
Surviving are his wife; one son, Maurice, San Bernadino, Calif.; one sister, Mrs. Frances Froid, St. Petersburg, Fla.; two grandchildren;and six great-grandchildren.
Services will be held at Lunning Chapel, Burlington, at 10 a.m. Friday. The Rev. Richard Gilbert will officiate with interment at Forest Home Cemetery. Masonic services will be held at 7 p.m. Thursday at the chapel with visitation following.
A memorial has been established for the Klein Hospital recreational department.
